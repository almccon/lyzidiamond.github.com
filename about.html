Hi!

I'm Lyzi Diamond, I'm 24 years old, and I live in Portland, Oregon. I'm glad you're here.



I'm a somewhat-recent University of Oregon Geography graduate working as a GIS Technician at the Oregon Department of Geology and Mineral Industries (DOGAMI). My work at DOGAMI is primarily on FEMA flood mapping/NFIP contracts as part of its new Risk MAP spec, doing a combination of spatial analysis, Python scripting, technical writing, and cartography. I am also doing some web development (JavaScript, HTML, CSS) to create a public gallery to showcase the agency's web map applications and services. In this capacity, I use both Esri tools and open source tools and libraries (Leaflet, TileMill/MapBox, and CartoDB).

I spend most of the rest of my time involved with women in tech organizations, in an official capacity as the Volunteer Coordinator for Code Scouts and also as a participant in PyLadies PDX. I have also done volunteer work for ChickTech, and I have given at least one talk about the importance of helping beginners to get excited about coding and development using git and GitHub.

I am a member of the Oregon URISA Young Professionals, where I help organize events and run the organization's Twitter account. I also helped to make the new website for the group, which is also powered on GitHub pages with Jekyll. I am also on the planning committee for the NW GIS User Group Conference to take place in October in Sunriver, Oregon; for the conference, I am coordinating a scholarship for young professionals and organizing volunteers for the event.

In my spare time, I play in the Chalice Bells handbell choir at the First Unitarian Church in Portland, I play cribbage (somewhat) competitively, I bake and decorate cakes and cupcakes, I race dragon boats, and I am generally working on at least one freelance mapping project and/or working my way through some coding tutorial. I also love college football, cooking, and riding my bicycle.

I like pretty much everything.
